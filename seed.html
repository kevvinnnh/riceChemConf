Seed Grants

The Rice University Office of the Vice Provost for Academic Affairs and the Office of Research have initiated a program to fund “seed grants” for new projects that support the university’s research and scholarship mission.

Seed grants provide funds to initiate projects, demonstrate feasibility of new ideas, and/or generate preliminary data for proposals to external sponsors. These grants are intended to promote collaborative projects between investigators from different disciplines, and to enable investigators to pursue new and creative ideas with potential for high impact.

Seed grant funds should not be used to supplement or extend existing grant projects, nor should they be used to purchase equipment or supplies for an existing research program. Grants are typically for one year, and the maximum award amount is $25,000.

Eligibility

Faculty members and research scientists at Rice University are eligible to apply for seed grants. The grants are intended to support projects that are in the early stages of development, and that are not yet ready for submission to external sponsors.

Application Process

The application process for seed grants involves submission of a brief (maximum three-page) proposal describing the research or scholarly project, including objectives, methods, and expected outcomes. Proposals must also include a detailed budget, and a curriculum vitae or biosketch for each participating investigator.

Proposals are reviewed by a committee of faculty members from a range of disciplines, and awards are made based on the potential impact of the proposed project, the feasibility of the proposed methods, and the qualifications of the participating investigators.
